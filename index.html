Our Story

One of Baton Rouge's oldest soul food kitchens.

Ethel's opened its doors in the mid-1950s and has been a fixture of the Scotlandville neighborhood near Southern University ever since. Around here, "snack" has always been code for something bigger — a full plate of honest, slow-cooked soul food.

Since 2012, Paulette Thomas has carried the tradition forward, cooking from recipes handed down by her mother — the same meals she once made for Paulette and her five brothers every night. In 2021 her son Roderick Brown joined her behind the counter, and together they keep the kitchen going the old way: everything made from scratch, every single day.

"You can't go out to McDonald's all the time. You got to cook." — Paulette Thomas, owner

2024 Soul Food Pioneer

Honored at the 7th Annual Baton Rouge Soul Food Festival for preserving and sharing local soul food traditions for nearly 70 years.
